Trading Rules

The mechanical rules and risk limits governing OptionsBot's two trading agents.

UOA Agent (Unusual Options Activity)

The UOA agent watches institutional options flow and opens swing-style positions when a signal passes both mechanical filters and Claude's qualitative scoring.

Signal source & filters

  • Source: Unusual Whales flow alerts, polled every 5 minutes during market hours (9:30–16:00 ET)
  • Minimum total premium: $250,000
  • Fill price ≥ 70% of the ask (paid-up flow only)
  • Volume must exceed open interest on the contract
  • DTE window: 7–730 days (covers short-term and LEAPS)
  • Both calls and puts; both ITM and OTM accepted

Claude scoring

  • Baseline 50%; trade threshold ≥74%
  • 70–73% → "Considered" alert (no order)
  • Below 70% → pass
  • DTE > 65 days routes the signal to the LEAPS evaluator instead
  • OTM penalty when underlying > $20: 10–20% OTM = −3%, >20% OTM = −5%
  • +5% confidence bonus if the same ticker had prior UOA flow in the last 5 trading days

Implied volatility (IV vs HV)

IV is priced against 30-day realized volatility (HV30). Cheap premium gets a bonus; expensive premium gets penalized for vol-crush risk.

  • Short-term signals:
    • IV/HV < 0.8x (cheap): +8%
    • IV/HV 0.8–1.3x (fair): 0%
    • IV/HV > 1.3x (expensive): −8%
    • IV unavailable: −5%
  • LEAPS: wider tolerance bands since there's more time for vol to normalize:
    • IV/HV < 1.0x (cheap): +6%
    • IV/HV 1.0–1.5x (fair): 0%
    • IV/HV > 1.5x (expensive): −6%
    • IV unavailable: −3%
  • Vega interaction: high vega (vega ÷ ask > 0.15) combined with expensive IV (> 1.3x HV) applies an additional −5% — vol-crush risk is amplified on high-vega contracts
  • Exit advisor also receives the live IV/HV reading on every cycle and factors it into the hold / trim / exit decision (e.g., a position with IV collapsing into a fair range is a candidate for trimming)

LEAPS-specific rules

  • Ask must be < $15.00 per contract to trigger an alert
  • +5% bonus if ask < $6.00
  • LEAPS signals flagged as "LEAP Considered" — alert only, no auto-order

Position sizing

  • Default: 2 contracts per order (minimum)
  • Up to 4 contracts on standard premium
  • Up to 8 contracts when ask ≤ $2.00
  • Max premium per trade: $2,000
  • One open position per ticker — duplicates rejected pre-order

Portfolio caps

  • Max 20 concurrent UOA positions
  • Max 40% of account equity allocated across all UOA positions
  • Max 5 new UOA trades opened per calendar day (a same-day close frees a slot)
  • Max absolute portfolio delta: 500
  • Max loss per trade: 8% of account value

Exit management

  • Hard stop-loss: position closes when down 50% from entry cost
  • Same-day stop-loss is suppressed — minimum one-day hold before stops can fire
  • Exit advisor: Claude evaluates every open position each cycle and may recommend exit, hold, or trim
  • Trim = sell half the contracts; the remainder stays monitored under the same rules

Day Trader Agent

The day trader runs in parallel on a 60-second loop, taking short-dated call and put positions on liquid index and high-volume tickers.

Universe

  • Index ETFs: SPY, QQQ, DIA, IWM
  • Sector / macro ETFs (chosen for low correlation with SPY/QQQ): SMH (semiconductors), XLF (financials), XLE (energy), TLT (20Y Treasuries), GDX (gold miners)
  • Single names with daily option chains: NVDA, TSLA, AAPL
  • Plus the top-10 most-active US tickers, refreshed every 30 minutes (de-duplicated against the base list above)

Signal ranking

When more than one ticker fires a passing signal in the same cycle, every candidate is evaluated by Claude first, then sorted by Claude's confidence score. Position slots are filled top-down: highest-confidence setup goes first. If the position or allocation cap is reached, the remaining lower-confidence candidates are recorded as deferred rather than executed. This prevents an alphabetically-earlier weak signal from filling a slot that a later, stronger signal could have used.

Expiry selection

For each entry the bot tries expiries in priority order: 0DTE first, then 1DTE, then the nearest listed expiry capped at 4DTE. The cap covers tickers like DIA, INTC, F, SOFI, IREN, QUBT that only list weekly Friday expirations — a Monday entry on such a ticker lands at 4DTE (that Friday), a Wednesday entry at 2DTE, and Thursday/Friday entries fall back to 0DTE only. The 4-day ceiling keeps the day trader on short-dated premium even when the chain has no daily expirations.

Entry signals (5-min candles)

  • ema_cross — fresh 9/21 EMA crossover, price on the correct side of VWAP, RSI between 25–75
  • trend_pull — EMA9 already meaningfully above/below EMA21, price pulled back to EMA9 and bounced; RSI in the trend band: 45–80 for calls, 20–55 for puts
  • Every passing signal is then evaluated by Claude for setup quality — Claude can veto on hairline crosses, doji bars, lunch-lull chop, etc.
  • Market Tide gate: blocks any direction that opposes broad market-wide flow

Time gates (Eastern Time)

  • Entry window: 09:45 – 14:30 (start was tightened from 09:35 to skip opening-bell chop)
  • Force-close all open day trades at: 15:45

Liquidity filters

  • Minimum option bid: $0.10
  • Maximum bid-ask spread: 30% of mid-price

Note: the day trader does not use implied volatility as an entry filter. Intraday 0DTE/1DTE setups are too short-dated for IV/HV ratios to be meaningful — the decision is driven by price action (EMA, VWAP, RSI), market tide, and gamma-exposure context instead.

Position sizing & caps

  • Max spend per trade: $1,000 (this is a ceiling, not a target — see even-lot rule below)
  • Max 5 concurrent day trade positions
  • Max 20% of account equity allocated across all open day trades
  • Confidence-scaled sizing (Claude's confidence on the setup):
    • ≥ 80% → full size, budget = $1,000
    • 70–79% → half size, budget = $500
    • < 70% → trade skipped entirely (logged as low-confidence)
  • Even-lot rounding — contract count must be even so the trim can sell half. The bot computes:
    • max_contracts = floor(budget ÷ (ask × 100))
    • contracts = max_contracts rounded DOWN to the nearest even number
    Actual notional = contracts × ask × 100, which is typically well under the budget. Examples at full size ($1,000 budget):
    • ask $4.50 → 2 contracts → $900
    • ask $3.80 → 2 contracts → $760
    • ask $2.60 → floor=3, rounds down to 2 → $520
    • ask $2.10 → 4 contracts → $840
    At half size the floor bites harder: a $500 budget at ask $3.20 rounds down to 0 even contracts and the trade is skipped (too expensive for 2 contracts log line).

Exit management

  • Hard stop-loss: −40% drawdown (pre-trim only)
  • Breakeven stop: once an untrimmed position has been up +25%, a hard floor is armed at entry — if price subsequently round-trips back to entry, the position is sold flat. Prevents pop-and-fade winners from rolling all the way back to the −40% stop.
  • Trim half at:
    • +50% gain for 0DTE positions (tightened — same-day premium decays too fast to wait for +75%)
    • +75% gain for 1+ DTE positions
  • After trim: runner rides until an EMA reversal or the 15:45 ET time-stop
  • EMA reversal exit: requires the reversal candle's body (|close − open|) to be at least 30% of the recent 14-bar ATR — filters out exits triggered by tiny indecision bars
  • 1DTE runners: if trimmed Monday–Thursday, the remainder may hold overnight; closed by Friday or whenever it becomes 0DTE

Order execution & P&L accounting

  • Buys: limit orders at mid + $0.05; runner waits up to 15s for Tradier to confirm a fill. If no fill confirms in time, the position is not recorded — a manual-reconcile alert is sent.
  • Sells (trim and close): market orders; runner waits up to 10s for the avg_fill_price.
  • All recorded entry / exit prices and realized P&L are based on Tradier's reported avg_fill_price, not the bot's pre-order mid-quote estimate. Decisions to stop / trim / close are still driven by the live mid (no fill exists yet at that moment), but everything persisted to the database reflects actual fills.

System-wide safeguards

  • PAPER_MODE default — orders only route live when explicitly disabled
  • KILL_SWITCH env flag halts all new orders immediately when set
  • Tradier sandbox handles paper routing; production endpoints supply market data (quotes, greeks, candles, chains) regardless of mode
  • All decisions, signals, and trades are persisted to SQLite for review

These rules describe the current configuration. Parameters are tuned over time; the page may not reflect the very latest commit.
